AegisAid

Privacy-Safe Humanitarian Resource AI

Designing a resource allocation and anomaly detection system for 120,000 displaced individuals that is simultaneously mathematically secure and structurally incapable of exposing identity.

The Dilemma

The Danger of Humanitarian Data

Humanitarian aid demands needs assessment (food index, healthcare requirements, shelter conditions). However, databases represent high-value targets.

A database breach can expose vulnerable refugees to exploitation, targeted deportation, or surveillance.

The Core Challenge

How do we compile and compute complex needs data across camps to optimize supplies distribution, while ensuring that the data cannot be reverse-engineered to identify individuals?

The Solution

AegisAid Architecture

  • k-Anonymity (Group Safety): Aggregates records into classes of size $\ge 15$, suppressing unique cohorts to prevent linkage attacks.
  • Differential Privacy (Laplace Blurring): Adds calibrated noise based on mathematical bounds, blocking reconstruction queries.
  • Decentralized Sandbox: Offline-first compilation ensures raw data stays on local devices, avoiding centralized leak vectors.
🛡️

Do-No-Harm Core

Moving from paper-based assessments to AI analytics with privacy proven mathematically, not just claimed.

Mathematical Proofs

DP & k-Anonymity Engine

By implementing Local Differential Privacy (LDP) using a Laplace mechanism, query outcomes are statistically blurred.

Reported_Sum = Actual_Sum + Laplace(Scale = Sensitivity / Epsilon)

When combined with a $k$-Anonymity constraint ($k=15$), linkage attack algorithms score **0% re-identification rate**, rendering data completely secure even under state surveillance.

Ethical Allocation

Fairness-Constrained Optimizer

Pure utility algorithms allocate resources only to regions that yield the highest numerical output, often under-serving minority subgroups.

We introduce a Fairness parameter (α) to balance proportional need-based allocation with egalitarian distributions.

Guaranteed Support

Ensures Camp Alpha (food crisis), Camp Beta (infrastructure collapse), and Camp Gamma (health outbreak) receive equitable support irrespective of camp demographic size.

Inclusivity

Low-Literacy Consent UI

Refugee camps host vulnerable populations with varying literacy levels. Traditional terms of service are unethical and ineffective.

  • Audio Guided Explanations: Multi-lingual voice assistance (Somali, Arabic, French, English).
  • Pictographic representation: Metaphors detailing data aggregation and privacy boundaries.
  • Dignity in Refusal: Opt-out records default to standard support baskets without penalization.
